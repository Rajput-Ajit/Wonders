Machu Picchu - Peru

Machu Picchu was built by the Inca civilization in the 15th century, during the reign of the Inca emperor Pachacuti. It is situated on a mountain ridge above the Sacred Valley of the Urubamba River, at an altitude of approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet).

Machu Picchu was relatively unknown to the outside world until its rediscovery by American historian and explorer Hiram Bingham in 1911 . He introduced it to the world through his book "The Lost City of the Incas." The exact purpose of Machu Picchu is still debated among historians and archaeologists. Some believe it served as a royal estate or a religious retreat, while others suggest it had astronomical and agricultural significance.

The site includes extensive agricultural terraces that were likely used for farming and experimentation with different crops at varying altitudes.

The Great Wall of china

The great wall of china is one of the most extensive construction projects in history, stretching over 13,000 miles(21,196 kilometers). It was build over several dynaties,with construction starting in the 7th century BC and contunuing into the 16th century.

The primary purpose of the Great Wall was not to keep out invading Monglos but to protect against raids and invasion by various nomadic groups and military incursion.

The idea that the Great Wall is visible from space with the naked eye is a common misconception. While it can be seen from low Earth orbit,it is not easly visible without aid.

Different chinese dynaties played roles in the construction and maintanance of the wall. Notably,significant portions were build during the Ming Dynasty.

The Great Wall is not a continuous wall but consists of multiple walls and fortifications. Different sections were build by Different dynaties and served various strategic purpose.

The Great Wall of china has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987.

Petra, Jordan

Petra was capital of the Nabatian Kingdom, a prosperous trading civilization that existed from the 4th century BC to the 1st century AD.

One of the most distinctive features of Petra is its elaborate rock-cut architecture. The city is known for its intricates structure carved directly into the rose-red cliffs of southern Jordan.

The most famous monument in Petra is Al-Khazneh, often referred to as "The Tresury".This well-preserved building in a mausoleum with a stunning faced,and its sereved as a tomb for a Nabataean King.

In 1985, Petra was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognixing its cultural significance.

Petra was "rediscovered" by the Western world in 1812 when a Swiss explore named John Ludwig Burckhardt disguised himself as a Bedouin and infiltrated the city.

Petra has served as a filming location for several movies, most notably in the film "Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade, " where Al-Khazneh was featured as the fictional "Canyon of the Crescent Moon."

Christ the Redeemer - Brazil

Christ the Redeemer is a colossal Art Deco Statue of Jejus Christ,standing atop the Corcova mountain in the Tijuca Forest National Park overlooking Rio de janeiro.

The statue itself is approximately 98 feet (30 meters) tall,and when including its pedestal , it reaches a height of about 125 feet (38 meters).

The statue was constructed between 1922 and 1931, designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva costa and sculpted by French artist Paul Landowski. The face was created by Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida.

The statue is a symbol of Christianity and is meant to represent Jesus Christ's role as a universal redeemer.

In 2012, Christ the Redeemer, along with the surrounding Tijuca National Park, was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The statue has undergone several restoration projects over the years to address weathering and damage caused by natural elements.

Machu Picchu - Peru

Machu Picchu was built by the Inca civilization in the 15th century, during the reign of the Inca emperor Pachacuti.

It is situated on a mountain ridge above the Sacred Valley of the Urubamba River, at an altitude of approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet).

Machu Picchu was relatively unknown to the outside world until its rediscovery by American historian and explorer Hiram Bingham in 1911 . He introduced it to the world through his book "The Lost City of the Incas."

The exact purpose of Machu Picchu is still debated among historians and archaeologists. Some believe it served as a royal estate or a religious retreat, while others suggest it had astronomical and agricultural significance.

The site includes extensive agricultural terraces that were likely used for farming and experimentation with different crops at varying altitudes.

In 1983, Machu Picchu was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in recognition of its cultural and historical significance.

Chichen Itza - Mexico

Chichen Itza was a major city of the ancient Maya civilization, and its ruins showcase a blend of Maya and Toltec architecture styles.

The most iconic structure at Chichen Itza is the Pyramid of Kukulkan, as step pyramid with 365 steps represting the solar year.

Chichen Itza played a significant role in the Mayan calender system, and various structures align with astronomical events, such as the equinoxes.

Chichen Itza features the largest and most well-preserved ancient ball court in Mesoamerica. The great ball court is known for its impressive acoustics and the largest stone rings set high on the walls through which players attempted to pass rubber balls.

The structure is the large stepped pyramid surrounded by numerous carved depictions of warriors found on the pillars.

Chichen Itza was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988, recognizing its cultural and historical significance.

Today, Chichen Itza is one of most visited archeological sites in Mexico, attracting tourists from around the world who come to explore its well-preserved ruins and learn about ancient Maya civilization.

Roman Colosseum - Itally

The construction of the colosseum began in AD 72 under the emperor Vespasian and was completed in AD 80 under his successor and heir, Titus. It was further modified during the reign of Domitian.

The Colosseum is the largest amphitheatre ever build and could hold as estimated 50,000 to 80,000 spectators. It is an elliptical structure with four stories, and its exterior is made of travertine limestone.

The Colosseum was primarily used for gladiatoial contests, animal hunts,execution , and various public spectacles. It served as a means of entertainment for the Roman people and was a symbol of imperial and power and grandeur.

Over the time, the Colosseum fell into a state of disrepair, and various natural disasters, including earthquakes, took their toll. Parts of the structure were later reused for the construction in Rome.

In the 18th century, various popes initiated restoration projects to stabilize and repair the Colosseum .Despite its parttial ruin, the colosseum remains a symbol of ancient Rome and a testament to Roman engineering and architectural prowess.

The Colosseum, along with other historic monument in Rome, is designated us a UNISCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural and historical significance.

Taj Mahal, India

The Taj mahal was build by the Mughal Emperor shah jahan in memory of his favourite wife, mumtaj mahal. Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653.

The taj mahal is a prime example of mugal archetecture, which combines elements of persian, turkish , indian and islamic architectural styles.

The main structure is made of white marble quarried from makrana in rajasthan. The intricate designs and patterns on the marble are created using precious and semi-precious stones.

The taj mahal is often considered a symbol of eternal love. the story behind its construction reflects the deep affection and grief of emperor shah jahan for his wife mumtaj mahal, who died during chilbirth.

The taj mahal is known for its perfect symmetry. The four minarets surrounding the main mausoleum are intentionally build slightly outward to create an optical illusion. This ensures that they appear straight when from a distance.

The color of taj mahal changes throught the day, appearing pinkish in the morning, milky white in the evening, and golden under the moonlight. This is due to the changing sunlight and the reflective properties of the marble.

The taj mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 in recognition of its cultural significnace and outstanding universal value.

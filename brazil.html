Christ the Redeemer - Brazil

Christ the Redeemer is a colossal Art Deco Statue of Jejus Christ,standing atop the Corcova mountain in the Tijuca Forest National Park overlooking Rio de janeiro.

The statue itself is approximately 98 feet (30 meters) tall,and when including its pedestal , it reaches a height of about 125 feet (38 meters). The statue was constructed between 1922 and 1931, designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva costa and sculpted by French artist Paul Landowski. The face was created by Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida.

The statue is a symbol of Christianity and is meant to represent Jesus Christ's role as a universal redeemer. In 2012, Christ the Redeemer, along with the surrounding Tijuca National Park, was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

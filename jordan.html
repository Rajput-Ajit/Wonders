Petra, Jordan

Petra was capital of the Nabatian Kingdom, a prosperous trading civilization that existed from the 4th century BC to the 1st century AD. One of the most distinctive features of Petra is its elaborate rock-cut architecture. The city is known for its intricates structure carved directly into the rose-red cliffs of southern Jordan.

The most famous monument in Petra is Al-Khazneh, often referred to as "The Tresury".This well-preserved building in a mausoleum with a stunning faced,and its sereved as a tomb for a Nabataean King. In 1985, Petra was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognixing its cultural significance. Petra was "rediscovered" by the Western world in 1812 when a Swiss explore named John Ludwig Burckhardt disguised himself as a Bedouin and infiltrated the city. Petra has served as a filming location for several movies, most notably in the film "Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade, " where Al-Khazneh was featured as the fictional "Canyon of the Crescent Moon."
